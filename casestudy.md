Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing
Phishing is an online scam where cybercriminals trick individuals into providing sensitive information, such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. It often occurs through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear legitimate.

Scenario: A college student receives an email claiming to be from their bank, asking them to verify account details urgently. The email contains a link that looks like the bank's official website. Thinking it is genuine, the student clicks the link and enters their login credentials.

Step-by-Step:

The attacker creates a fake email mimicking a trusted source.
The email urges immediate action, creating panic.
The student clicks the link and submits sensitive information.
The attacker uses this information to access the student’s account.
Target: Students and young professionals who may not recognize suspicious emails.

Consequences: Loss of money, personal data theft, and potential identity misuse. Victims may also face stress and reputation damage.
